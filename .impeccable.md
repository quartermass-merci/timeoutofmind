## Design Context

### Users
Independent bands, touring musicians, labels/managers evaluating the space, and curious locals who attend barn shows or are part of the Essex County arts community. They're arriving via word-of-mouth, social media, or a link from a booking agent. Context: likely on a phone or laptop, deciding whether this place is worth the drive/investment. The job to be done: "Is this the real deal? Will my band have a great experience here?"

### Brand Personality
**Raw. Authentic. Defiant.**

The voice is confident and opinionated without being aggressive. Anti-autotune, anti-phone, pro-vibe. It's the friend who runs a recording studio in a 100-year-old barn and means it when they say "lock your phone in the mic locker." The tone is warm but has edge — think independent record label, not corporate music-tech.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Found-object analog. Typewriter text, parchment textures, barn-wood warmth. The site should feel like it was made by hand, not generated.
- **Color palette**: Earthy — parchment (#EDE4D3), ink (#1C1610), barn (#2A1F14), amber (#C4956A), night (#12100D). No blues, no tech colors, no neon.
- **Typography**: Special Elite (typewriter display) + Courier Prime (monospace body). The monospace stack is intentional — it evokes typed session notes, not code.
- **Reference**: robmoir.com — clean, minimal, content-forward, photography-driven.
- **Anti-references**: Splice/Ableton (slick music-tech, dark + neon). Generic Squarespace band templates (interchangeable, stock-photo energy). Overdesigned boutique studios (marble, gold, luxury-hotel aesthetic). None of these. This place is a barn with a console and a cat.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. The warmth of the parchment IS the brand.

### Design Principles
1. **The space sells itself** — Photography and real content do the heavy lifting. The design gets out of the way and lets the barn, the console, the countryside speak.
2. **Analog over digital** — Every design choice should feel tactile: typewriter fonts, paper grain, desaturated photography, amber accents like aged wood. Never slick, never glossy.
3. **Confidence through restraint** — No need to oversell. Small section labels, generous whitespace, unhurried pacing. The site scrolls like a slow afternoon in the country.
4. **Personality in the details** — The coda quotes, the tap-dancing facilities manager, the communal copy of *Our Band Could Be Your Life*. Let these moments land — don't bury them in visual noise.
5. **Photography breaks the grid** — Photos should interrupt the reading flow naturally, not sit in predictable boxes. They're the texture of the experience, woven through the content.
